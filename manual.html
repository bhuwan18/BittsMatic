BitsMatic

A browser-based factory automation puzzle game. Route numbers through conveyor belts and math machines to feed the CORE the exact value it demands.

30 Levels 10 Machine Types Upgrade System Blueprint Copy/Paste

🎯Overview

BittsMatic is a factory-building puzzle game where numbers are the product. Your factory sits on a 150×150 grid. Resource nodes on the grid emit raw numbers; you build conveyor belts to route those numbers through mathematical machines — Add, Multiply, Divide, and more — to transform them into the exact target value demanded by the CORE.

Deliver the correct value to the CORE to complete the current objective, earn upgrade points, and unlock new machines. All 30 objectives must be completed in sequence to finish the game.

Core Loop Extract numbers from nodes → Route along belts → Transform with math machines → Deliver to the CORE → Advance to the next objective.

🚀Quick Start

Your First Factory

Here is the fastest path from an empty grid to your first CORE delivery:

  1. Press 2 to select the Extractor tool and click a resource node (pulsing circle on the grid) to place one.
  2. Press 1 to select the Belt tool. Left-click and drag from the Extractor toward the CORE (the large 3×3 machine at the center) to draw a belt line.
  3. Numbers should now flow from the node along your belt. Watch the items (small colored squares) travel toward the CORE.
  4. The first objective target is 1. Each node emits 1 by default — deliver it directly to complete level 1.
  5. Complete the delivery to earn your first upgrade point and advance to level 2.
Tip Right-click any placed machine or belt to rotate its output direction. Rotation determines which direction items will leave the machine.

🖥️Interface Tour

Objective Banner

Shown at the top of the screen. Displays the current level number and the exact value you must deliver to the CORE. Targets are randomized ±20% around a base sequence each game, so every playthrough is slightly different.

Toolbar

Lists all currently unlocked tools. Click a button or press its hotkey (1–0) to select it. The active tool is highlighted. New tools appear here automatically when you reach their unlock level.

Inventory

Shows every unique number value you have delivered to the CORE so far, along with a delivery count per value. Useful for tracking your throughput and resource variety.

Milestones

A list of upcoming machine unlocks with their required level. Locked entries are greyed out; completed ones are checked. Use this to plan ahead which machines you will have available soon.

Upgrades

Displays your current upgrade point balance and all purchasable upgrades. Shows each upgrade's current tier, cost to advance, and the level required to purchase. Greyed-out upgrades are either unaffordable or level-locked.

Blueprint Status

When a region has been copied, the panel shows the entity count and dimensions of the clipboard. During paste mode it shows that paste is active.

Math Machines

All math machines share the same basic design: a 1×2 footprint (vertical by default, horizontal via blueprint rotation) with two input slots — A (top/left) and B (bottom/right) — and a single configurable output direction. 🔁Belts & Bridges

Belt Basics

Each belt tile holds one item at a time and carries it in a single direction. Items move along a normalized offset (0 = just arrived, 1 = ready to hand off). When an item reaches offset 1 it attempts to transfer to the next tile; if that tile is occupied or blocked the item waits.

Bridges

When you draw a belt that crosses an existing belt perpendicularly, the game automatically upgrades that intersection tile to a Bridge (unlocked at level 13). A Bridge is a two-layer tile: each layer independently carries items in its original direction without interference. Items on the horizontal layer and vertical layer never interact.

Tip — Compact routing You do not need to plan around intersections. Simply draw belts freely and bridges form wherever needed. This keeps factory layouts compact.

Belt Speed

The global Belt Speed upgrade multiplier is applied to all belts simultaneously. A higher tier means items travel faster, increasing the throughput of your whole factory without requiring more machines.

📋Blueprints

Blueprints let you copy a region of your factory and paste it anywhere — including a rotated version. This is essential for duplicating proven sub-factories.

Workflow

  1. Press 8 or Ctrl+drag to activate the Select tool, then drag over the region you want to copy.
  2. Press Ctrl+C to copy or Ctrl+X to cut. The Blueprint Status panel confirms the copy (entity count and dimensions).
  3. Press Ctrl+V to enter paste mode. A translucent preview follows your cursor.
  4. Optionally press R to rotate the blueprint 90° before placing. Belt directions and machine orientations rotate automatically.
  5. Click to stamp the blueprint at the desired location. Press Esc to cancel.

Paste Validation A blueprint cannot be placed if any required tile is already occupied by an incompatible machine. An invalid location shows the preview in red. Bridge auto-creation still applies during paste.

🏷️Markers

Markers are text annotations you can pin anywhere on the grid to label factory regions, note target values, or leave reminders.

Placing a Marker

  1. Select a region using the Select tool (8 or Ctrl+drag).
  2. Press B. A dialog will appear asking for a text label (max 32 characters).
  3. Type your label and confirm. The marker appears at the top-left corner of your selection, rendered in yellow (#ffe066) by default.

Markers are saved with your game state and restored automatically on next load. They are not included in blueprint copies but they persist on the grid independently.

📊Statistics

The game tracks real-time performance metrics for every machine using a 60-frame rolling window.

MetricDescription
ThroughputAverage items produced per frame over the last 60 frames for a given machine.
EfficiencyRatio of productive frames to total frames: production / (production + blocked). A value of 1.0 means the machine never stalls.
Core DeliveriesCumulative count of items accepted by the CORE (value matched the objective).
Wrong DeliveriesCumulative count of items rejected by the CORE (value did not match).

Viewing Stats

Hover your mouse over any machine on the canvas. A tooltip appears above the machine showing its current throughput and efficiency values. Use this to identify bottlenecks: a machine with low efficiency is blocking frequently — either its input belts are undersupplied or its output is congested.

💡Tips & Strategies

Bonus Tip — Completing Level 1

Level 1 asks for a value of 1 (±20%, so the target will be exactly 1 at the low end — and extractors emit 1 by default). This means you can complete it with the absolute minimum setup:

  1. Find a resource node. Zoom out if needed (scroll down) to spot the pulsing circles on the grid near the CORE.
  2. Place an Extractor. Press 2, then click the node. The Extractor immediately starts emitting value-1 items every 2 ticks.
  3. Draw a belt to the CORE. Press 1, then left-click and drag a continuous belt path from the Extractor's output toward any open side of the CORE (the large 3×3 machine). You do not need math machines for this level.
  4. Check the Extractor's output direction. If items are not flowing toward the CORE, right-click the Extractor to rotate it until its arrow points along your belt.
  5. Wait for delivery. A value-1 item will travel the belt and enter the CORE. The objective banner updates, a success toast appears, and you advance to level 2 with 1 upgrade point earned.
Common stumbling block If nothing moves, the Extractor output arrow is pointing away from the belt. Right-click the Extractor once or twice until the arrow faces the first belt tile you placed. Items will start flowing immediately.

Early Game (Levels 1–5)

  • Place extractors on every nearby node — even ones you do not immediately need. More inputs = more flexibility.
  • Use Add machines to double a stream: feed the same line into both A and B inputs via a split.
  • Subtract unlocks alongside Add at level 1 — useful for fine-tuning values even in the early game.
  • Deliver directly to the CORE for the early single-digit targets; machines are overhead you do not yet need.

Mid Game (Levels 4–18)

  • Multiply unlocks at level 4 — immediately build a 1 × 1 = 1 chain then upgrade to n × n to reach targets like 64, 128.
  • Buy Extractor Speed at level 5 before anything else — it doubles production rate cheaply.
  • Divide arrives at level 10 with a bonus remainder output; route the remainder back into your factory to avoid waste.
  • Use blueprints to duplicate your successful doubling chains rather than rebuilding from scratch.
  • Bridges unlock at level 13 — until then, plan belt routes to avoid crossings.

Late Game (Levels 19–30)

  • Exponentiate is the key to targets like 2048 (2^11), 1296 (6^4), 729 (3^6). Plan the base and exponent streams carefully.
  • Storage machines smooth out timing mismatches between arms of a pipeline — particularly useful when one math chain is slower than another.
  • Divide's remainder output is a free by-product — route it somewhere useful rather than letting it block.
  • By this stage bridges (unlocked level 13) are available — use them freely to keep your layout compact.
  • Belt Speed tier 3 (4×) combined with Extractor Speed tier 3 (2.5×) gives enormous throughput — buy these before tackling the high-value final objectives.
Power-of-two shortcut Many late targets are powers of 2 (512, 1024, 2048). Chain three Multiply machines each doubling a stream to get ×8 amplification in three steps, or use Exponentiate with base 2 and exponent derived from your extractor chain.
